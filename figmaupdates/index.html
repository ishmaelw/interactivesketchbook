Short Reflection — Structural Decisions

The main structural decision I made in my Figma wireframe was to move away from a simple list of links and instead design a system that clearly communicates navigation, hierarchy, and system status. Before, my homepage just linked to different sketchbook and micro-project HTML pages, which worked on desktop but did not really guide the user or show where they were in the system.

One important decision was adding a persistent navigation system using a sidebar on desktop. This makes movement predictable and keeps the structure visible at all times. Instead of users guessing where to go next, they can see the full organization of the sketchbook and move between sections intentionally. I also grouped content into cards instead of plain text links. That decision helped create consistent interaction cues, so every entry behaves the same way and feels like part of a larger system. Another key structural decision was introducing a visible system status area. This section shows the current page, phase, and interaction state, which helps users understand where they are and what is happening in the system. It reinforces the idea that my sketchbook is not just a collection of pages, but an evolving process over time. Overall, these decisions were focused on making the structure legible, improving navigation, and clearly communicating how the system works rather than just how it looks.
